Curry

Description

Japanese-style curry is my go-to recipe to make when I want a nice home-cooked meal for low effort. It has a lot of flavor, is very filling, and makes 8-10 servings. Leftovers are great with rice or ramen noodles.

Ingredients

  • 1lb meat (chicken, stew beef, etc.)
  • 2 large onions
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp ginger
  • 2 carrots
  • 3 medium red or yellow potatoes
  • salt & pepper to taste
  • oil
  • Java Curry curry block
  • 8 cups chicken stock
  • short grain white rice

Add-ins

  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Worcester sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp S&B curry powder
  • lemon juice

Steps

  1. Chop onions, garlic, carrots, and potatoes, and grate ginger. Put them to the side.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pot on medium heat.
  3. Chop the meat and cover in salt and pepper.
  4. Add meat and saute until at least the outside is fully cooked. It will be slow-cooked so try not to overdo it.
  5. Add onions and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add garlic, ginger, carrots, and potatoes.
  7. Add stock to the pot and turn the heat to high. Once it boils, skim the fat with a mesh skimmer (optional if lazy), and reduce heat to low. Let it cook for 45 minutes.
  8. Start desired amount of rice in the rice cooker. ~1/2 rice cooker cup per person, in my experience.
  9. Once it's been 45 minutes, turn up the heat to medium/medium-high and add curry block broken into chunks, as well as any desired add-ins. Save the lemon juice for later, though. Simmer for 10 minutes and stir regularly.
  10. Squeeze in lemon juice at the end, and stir together one last time. Serve with rice.
